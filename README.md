# Financial Loan Data Analysis 

This dataset contains information about loan applications, borrower characteristics, and loan repayment performance. <br> It includes details such as employment information, credit history, loan amounts, repayment schedules, and loan status. <br> This data can be used for tasks like credit risk analysis, predicting loan default, and understanding borrower behavior.

<h4> key Columns </h4>
1. loan_id – Unique identifier; acts as the primary key of the dataset.<br>
2. address_state – State where the customer resides.<br>
3. application_type – Type of loan application (Individual or Partnership). <br>
4. emp_length – Number of years the customer has been working in their current company. <br>
5. emp_title – Position or job title of the customer in the organization. <br>
6. grade – Grade level assigned to the loan.<br>
7. home_ownership – Customer’s home ownership status (e.g., Rent, Mortgage, Own). <br>
8. issue_date – Date when the loan was issued.<br>
9. last_credit_pull_date – Most recent date when the customer’s credit history was checked.<br>
10. last_payment_date – Date of the customer’s latest payment.<br>
11. loan_status – Status of the loan (e.g., Fully Paid, Late, Default).<br>
12. next_payment_date – Due date of the next EMI.<br>
13. member_id – Unique ID of the customer.<br>
14. purpose – Reason for taking the loan.<br>
15. sub_grade – Sub-grade level of the loan.<br>
16. term – Tenure or duration of the loan.<br>
17. verification_status – Indicates whether the customer’s documents were verified.<br>
18. annual_income – Customer’s annual income.<br>
19. dti – Debt-to-income ratio.<br>
20. installment – Monthly EMI amount.<br>
21. int_rate – Interest rate of the loan.<br>
22. loan_amount – Principal amount of the loan.
23. total_acc – Total number of accounts held by the customer.
24 total_payment – Total amount received from the customer.

<h3>Key Terminologies used :-</h3> 

<b>1.	Month-to-Date</b> :- Performance or total up to the current date in the current Month . 
Formula :- MTD = Sum(Values from 1st of current month) 
Example :- if everyday income is 2$ per day . and today is 8th Novmebr then MTD will be 16$.

<br>
<br>

<b>2.	Month-to-Month </b>:- Change or growth from last month to this month.
Formula :- MTM = (Current Month Value – Previous Month Value ) / Previous Month Value * 100 % 
Example:- If January Sales = 10k and Feb are 12k then MTM = (12-10k)/10k *100= 20%

<br><br>

<b> 3.	Debt-to-Income </b>  :- Measures how much of your income goes toward paying debts.
Formula :- DTI = Total Month Debts Payments / Gross Income  *  100%.
Example :- DTI=(30k/100k)*100 = 30 %
